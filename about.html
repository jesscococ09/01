What is Telemedicine Technologies?

Telemedicine refers to providing medical care through digital communication rather than in‑person visits. It helps people receive support when they need it, especially those who have trouble reaching traditional healthcare services. It also makes it possible to get a doctor’s guidance for non‑urgent issues without having to go into a clinic.

How does it realate to Compuer Science?

Telemedicine = Computer Science + Healthcare
Telemedicine Technologies connects to computer science through the digital systems that make remote care possible. Modern telemedicine relies on software engineering, secure data transmission, networking, and database management to support virtual appointments, electronic records, and remote monitoring devices. These services function through computer based platforms that handle communication, encryption, and realtime data processing, all of which are core areas within computer science. As a result, telemedicine is fundamentally a computing driven field that applies Computer Science principles to deliver healthcare at a distance.
